6:T1956,<h2>What FAQ Schema Actually Does (and What It Doesn&#39;t)</h2>
<p>When someone searches &quot;how much does Botox cost near me,&quot; Google doesn&#39;t just rank pages — it looks for structured signals that say: <em>this page directly answers that question.</em> FAQ schema is one of those signals.</p>
<p>Technically, it&#39;s a block of JSON-LD code added to your page that wraps your questions and answers in a format machines can parse without guessing. Here&#39;s a minimal example of what it looks like:</p>
<pre><code class="language-json">{
  &quot;@context&quot;: &quot;https://schema.org&quot;,
  &quot;@type&quot;: &quot;FAQPage&quot;,
  &quot;mainEntity&quot;: [
    {
      &quot;@type&quot;: &quot;Question&quot;,
      &quot;name&quot;: &quot;How long does Botox last?&quot;,
      &quot;acceptedAnswer&quot;: {
        &quot;@type&quot;: &quot;Answer&quot;,
        &quot;text&quot;: &quot;Botox typically lasts 3–4 months for most patients, though first-timers may metabolize it faster.&quot;
      }
    }
  ]
}
</code></pre>
<p>Simple. Human-readable. And exactly what AI engines want.</p>
<p>One honest note: Google pulled back FAQ rich results significantly in 2023. They now limit the expandable Q&amp;A snippets in search to government and health-focused sites. So if you were hoping for those accordion-style boxes in the SERPs, it&#39;s mostly not happening for med spas anymore.</p>
<p>That said, the schema is still worth adding. AI search tools — Perplexity, ChatGPT with browsing, Google&#39;s AI Overviews — actively parse structured data to identify authoritative Q&amp;A content. If your FAQ schema is clean and your answers are specific, you&#39;re giving those engines a roadmap to cite your practice instead of your competitor down the street.</p>
<hr>
<h2>Which Questions Should a Med Spa Actually Mark Up?</h2>
<p>Not every FAQ is worth the effort. You want to mark up questions that:</p>
<ol>
<li>Match what patients are actively searching</li>
<li>Have specific, confident answers (not &quot;it depends&quot;)</li>
<li>Live on a high-traffic or high-intent page</li>
</ol>
<p>For most med spas, the best candidates are:</p>
<p><strong>Pricing questions</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>&quot;How much does CoolSculpting cost near me?&quot;</li>
<li>&quot;What&#39;s the average price for a lip filler treatment?&quot;</li>
<li>&quot;Do you offer payment plans for laser packages?&quot;</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Pain and comfort questions</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>&quot;Does microneedling hurt?&quot;</li>
<li>&quot;Is laser hair removal painful?&quot;</li>
<li>&quot;What does a HydraFacial feel like?&quot;</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Downtime and recovery questions</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>&quot;How long is the recovery after RF microneedling?&quot;</li>
<li>&quot;Can I go back to work after Botox?&quot;</li>
<li>&quot;When can I wear makeup after a chemical peel?&quot;</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Results timelines</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>&quot;How long until I see results from Sculptra?&quot;</li>
<li>&quot;How many sessions of laser hair removal do I need?&quot;</li>
</ul>
<p>These are the questions patients type before booking. If your pages answer them clearly and schema confirms that, you&#39;re ahead.</p>
<hr>
<h2>How to Add It (Without Breaking Anything)</h2>
<h3>WordPress sites</h3>
<p>If your site runs on WordPress, this is straightforward. Both Yoast SEO Premium and Rank Math Pro have dedicated FAQ blocks in the editor. You write the question and answer in the block, and the plugin generates the JSON-LD automatically. No code required.</p>
<h3>Webflow, Squarespace, or custom builds</h3>
<p>You&#39;ll need to paste a JSON-LD script tag into the <code>&lt;head&gt;</code> of the relevant page. Your developer can do this in under 20 minutes per page. If you&#39;re on Webflow, custom code embeds work fine.</p>
<h3>After adding it</h3>
<p>Always validate with <a href="https://search.google.com/test/rich-results">Google&#39;s Rich Results Test</a>. It&#39;ll tell you if the schema is valid, flag any errors, and show you what (if anything) might appear in search. Even if no rich result triggers, you want to confirm the code is clean.</p>
<hr>
<h2>The One Pitfall That Will Get You Penalized</h2>
<p>Google&#39;s guidelines are clear: the content in your FAQ schema must be visible on the page. If you mark up a question and answer that doesn&#39;t actually appear in the body of the page, that&#39;s a policy violation.</p>
<p>This catches a surprising number of sites that add schema as a hidden layer while the visible page says something vague. Don&#39;t do it. Write the actual Q&amp;A in your page copy, then wrap it in schema. That approach is both compliant and genuinely useful to readers — two things that tend to go together.</p>
<hr>
<h2>Why This Connects to AI Visibility</h2>
<p>AI engines are increasingly the first stop for patients with questions. Someone might ask ChatGPT &quot;what&#39;s a realistic Botox budget for forehead lines&quot; before they ever type a keyword into Google. Those engines synthesize answers from sources they deem credible — and clean, well-structured content that uses FAQ schema is a strong credibility signal.</p>
<p>This is a core piece of what&#39;s called <a href="/blog/what-is-generative-engine-optimization">generative engine optimization</a> — optimizing not just for search rankings, but for the AI-generated answers patients see first. FAQ schema is one of the most concrete technical steps you can take in that direction.</p>
<p>It pairs well with <a href="/blog/answer-first-content-ai-citations">answer-first content</a>: leading your pages with a direct answer before expanding into detail. Schema marks up the answers; the content structure makes them trustworthy.</p>
<hr>

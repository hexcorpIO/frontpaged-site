1c:T24ff,<h2>Why Your GBP Is More Important Than Your Website Right Now</h2>
<p>Most patients searching for a med spa in their area never scroll past the map pack. They see three listings, read the star rating, scan the photos, and book. Your Google Business Profile is what gets you into that map pack and what converts the click once you&#39;re there.</p>
<p>The good news: most med spas in any local market have poorly optimized profiles. Half don&#39;t have their services filled in. Plenty have a handful of blurry photos from 2022. Some haven&#39;t responded to a review in months. If you work through this checklist, you&#39;ll be ahead of the competition before you spend a dollar on ads.</p>
<hr>
<h2>1. Profile Basics</h2>
<p><strong>Complete every field. No exceptions.</strong></p>
<ul>
<li><input disabled="" type="checkbox"> <strong>Claim and verify your listing</strong> if you haven&#39;t already. Go to business.google.com and verify via postcard, phone, or video.</li>
<li><input disabled="" type="checkbox"> <strong>Business name:</strong> Use your real business name only. Don&#39;t keyword-stuff it (e.g., &quot;Glow Med Spa Botox Near Me&quot;) — Google will suspend listings for this.</li>
<li><input disabled="" type="checkbox"> <strong>Address:</strong> Confirm it matches your signage, website, and any other directories exactly. A mismatch here can hurt your local ranking.</li>
<li><input disabled="" type="checkbox"> <strong>Phone number:</strong> Use a local area code, not an 800 number. Consistency across the web matters.</li>
<li><input disabled="" type="checkbox"> <strong>Website URL:</strong> Link to your homepage or a landing page — not your Facebook profile.</li>
<li><input disabled="" type="checkbox"> <strong>Hours:</strong> Set them accurately. Add holiday hours ahead of major dates (New Year&#39;s, Valentine&#39;s Day, etc.) — med spas see seasonal spikes in inquiries.</li>
<li><input disabled="" type="checkbox"> <strong>Business description:</strong> You get 750 characters. Use it. Mention your location (&quot;serving [your city] and surrounding areas&quot;), your key treatments, and what makes your practice different. Work in your primary keyword naturally but don&#39;t stuff it.</li>
</ul>
<hr>
<h2>2. Categories and Services</h2>
<p><strong>This is where most med spas leave ranking potential on the table.</strong></p>
<ul>
<li><input disabled="" type="checkbox"> <strong>Primary category:</strong> Set it to <strong>Medical spa</strong>. This is the single most important category signal.</li>
<li><input disabled="" type="checkbox"> <strong>Secondary categories:</strong> Add relevant ones based on what you actually offer:<ul>
<li>Skin care clinic</li>
<li>Laser hair removal service</li>
<li>Weight loss service</li>
<li>Medical aesthetics center</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><input disabled="" type="checkbox"> <strong>Services:</strong> Fill in every treatment you provide. For each one, include:<ul>
<li>Service name (e.g., &quot;Botox,&quot; &quot;Dermal Fillers,&quot; &quot;CoolSculpting&quot;)</li>
<li>A 2-3 sentence description explaining the treatment, who it&#39;s for, and what results to expect</li>
<li>Price or a price range if you can (optional, but it filters serious inquirers)</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<p>If you want to <a href="/blog/how-to-rank-for-botox-near-me">rank for &#39;Botox near me&#39;</a>, Botox needs to be listed as a service with its own description — not buried in your general business description.</p>
<hr>
<h2>3. Photos and Videos</h2>
<p><strong>Visuals drive bookings. A sparse photo section costs you patients every day.</strong></p>
<ul>
<li><input disabled="" type="checkbox"> <strong>Cover photo:</strong> Choose a clean, well-lit image of your front desk or lobby. This is the first thing people see in the map pack.</li>
<li><input disabled="" type="checkbox"> <strong>Logo:</strong> Upload a high-res version of your logo for brand recognition.</li>
<li><input disabled="" type="checkbox"> <strong>Exterior photos:</strong> At least 2, showing your entrance from the street during the day.</li>
<li><input disabled="" type="checkbox"> <strong>Interior photos:</strong> Treatment rooms, your waiting area, any equipment worth showing (laser devices, IV stations, body contouring machines).</li>
<li><input disabled="" type="checkbox"> <strong>Staff photos:</strong> Faces build trust. Include your lead injector, aesthetician, and front desk team.</li>
<li><input disabled="" type="checkbox"> <strong>Before/after results:</strong> Check your state board guidelines before posting. You&#39;ll want a clear disclaimer regardless of your state. These images drive more engagement than anything else.</li>
<li><input disabled="" type="checkbox"> <strong>Add new photos every month.</strong> Google rewards active profiles. A batch of 5 new photos per month takes 20 minutes and signals that you&#39;re open and operating.</li>
</ul>
<p>Skip stock photos. Patients can tell, and they don&#39;t convert.</p>
<hr>
<h2>4. Reviews</h2>
<p><strong>Your star rating and review volume are the most visible trust signals in local search.</strong></p>
<ul>
<li><input disabled="" type="checkbox"> <strong>Reach 50+ reviews</strong> as your first milestone. Below that, you&#39;re invisible to skeptical searchers.</li>
<li><input disabled="" type="checkbox"> <strong>Request reviews at the right moment:</strong> Ask right after a successful appointment, while the patient is still glowing (literally). A two-step works well: confirm they&#39;re happy in person, then send a direct link to your GBP review form.</li>
<li><input disabled="" type="checkbox"> <strong>Add a QR code</strong> at your front desk and check-out counter that links directly to your review page.</li>
<li><input disabled="" type="checkbox"> <strong>Respond to every review</strong> — positive and negative — within 24 hours. For positive reviews, thank them and mention the treatment by name (&quot;So glad you&#39;re loving your Sculptra results!&quot;). For negative ones, stay professional, take it offline, and show potential patients that you address concerns directly.</li>
<li><input disabled="" type="checkbox"> <strong>Mention specific treatments in your review requests.</strong> A review that says &quot;the laser hair removal was completely painless&quot; does more for your local rankings than a generic &quot;great spa!&quot; Review text with treatment keywords reinforces your relevance. This is one of the clearest ways <a href="/blog/patient-reviews-and-visibility">how reviews drive visibility</a> in local search.</li>
</ul>
<hr>
<h2>5. Google Posts</h2>
<p><strong>Most med spas ignore this feature. That&#39;s an opportunity for you.</strong></p>
<ul>
<li><input disabled="" type="checkbox"> <strong>Post at least once per week.</strong> Use these formats:<ul>
<li><strong>Offer:</strong> Seasonal promotions, first-time patient specials, bundled treatment discounts</li>
<li><strong>What&#39;s New:</strong> Spotlights on new treatments, provider introductions, equipment upgrades</li>
<li><strong>Event:</strong> Open houses, consultation events, free skin analysis days</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><input disabled="" type="checkbox"> <strong>Include a call to action</strong> on every post: &quot;Book now,&quot; &quot;Call to schedule,&quot; or &quot;Claim this offer.&quot;</li>
<li><input disabled="" type="checkbox"> <strong>Use local context.</strong> &quot;Now offering PRF treatments at our [your city] location&quot; beats a generic post every time.</li>
<li><input disabled="" type="checkbox"> <strong>Add a photo to every post.</strong> Engagement rates drop significantly on text-only posts.</li>
</ul>
<p>Posts expire after 7 days (except Events). Build this into your weekly routine.</p>
<hr>
<h2>6. Q&amp;A Section</h2>
<p><strong>Seed it before someone else does.</strong></p>
<ul>
<li><input disabled="" type="checkbox"> <strong>Log into your profile and post the questions you get asked most.</strong> Then answer them clearly and accurately. Good starting points for a med spa:<ul>
<li>&quot;Do I need a consultation before getting Botox?&quot;</li>
<li>&quot;How much does laser hair removal cost?&quot;</li>
<li>&quot;Is there any downtime after filler injections?&quot;</li>
<li>&quot;Do you accept CareCredit or payment plans?&quot;</li>
<li>&quot;Where are you located and is there parking nearby?&quot;</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><input disabled="" type="checkbox"> <strong>Check the Q&amp;A section weekly.</strong> Flag any inaccurate community answers and post your own corrected version.</li>
<li><input disabled="" type="checkbox"> <strong>Upvote your own seeded questions</strong> when logged in as a Google user — it pushes them to the top.</li>
</ul>
<hr>
<h2>Keep It Moving</h2>
<p>A well-optimized GBP isn&#39;t a one-time project. The practices that dominate their local map pack treat their profile like a channel: fresh photos, weekly posts, fast review responses, and updated hours before every holiday. It compounds over time.</p>

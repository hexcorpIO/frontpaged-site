12 Med Spa Blog Topics That Actually Book Consultations

Quick answer

The blog topics that actually book consultations are the ones that answer the exact questions people ask right before they decide to buy — cost, comparisons, what to expect, and recovery timelines. For any med spa, that means posts like 'How much does Botox cost near me?' and 'Botox vs Dysport: which lasts longer?' Those questions have clear buyer intent, and content that answers them directly earns both search rankings and AI citations.

The Blog Topics That Move the Needle

Most med spa blogs exist. Very few actually book anyone.

The gap is usually intent. If your posts are written for someone who is curious about injectables, you'll get readers. If they're written for someone who has already decided they want Botox and is now comparing providers, clinics, and prices in their area — you'll get consultations.

Here are 12 specific blog topics that do the second thing. Each one captures a question real patients type into Google (and increasingly into ChatGPT and Perplexity) right before they call a clinic.

Pair this with answer-first content and your med spa SEO fundamentals, and you have a content strategy that actually compounds.

Cost Posts — The Highest-Intent Traffic You Can Capture

People who search for prices have already decided they want something. They're not window shopping; they're vetting providers. Show up here and you're in the final consideration set.

1. "How much does Botox cost near me?" This query captures patients mid-decision across every market. Write a real answer with current price ranges by unit and treatment area, not a vague "prices vary" non-answer. Be specific. Clinics that give real numbers get more calls than clinics that hide them.

2. "How much does lip filler cost in your city?" Lip filler is one of the most searched cosmetic treatments for patients under 40. A city-specific post competes in a much smaller pool than a generic post and draws patients who are geographically close and actively comparing.

3. "What does a full syringe of Juvederm cost vs Restylane?" Brand comparisons within a treatment category hit patients who already understand fillers and are now making a product decision. These readers are close to booking. Give them a real comparison and you eliminate the need for them to call three other clinics to ask the same question.

Comparison Posts — Capture the 'Which Is Better' Searches

Comparison content works because it answers the question that comes right before the appointment. Someone researching these topics is nearly decided — they just want confirmation they're making the right call.

4. "Botox vs Dysport: which lasts longer?" This is one of the most-searched injectable questions nationwide. Both are neuromodulators; the real differences are onset speed, spread, and cost per unit. A post that explains this clearly — without selling one over the other — reads as credible and gets cited by AI engines. See our guide on FAQ schema for med spas to mark it up correctly.

5. "RF microneedling vs traditional microneedling: is the upgrade worth it?" Patients who are already sold on microneedling next ask whether they need the radiofrequency add-on. A post that honestly compares results, downtime, and cost range positions you as the provider who gives straight answers — which is what books consultations.

6. "Kybella vs CoolSculpting for under-chin fat: which one is right for you?" Body contouring comparison searches spike in January and before summer. A post that walks through candidacy, number of sessions, and realistic outcomes gives patients a clear framework for their decision — and puts your clinic front-of-mind when they're ready to move forward.

Treatment-Prep and Recovery Posts — The 'What to Expect' Search

These posts catch patients right after they've decided to book. They search to calm nerves and prepare. Your content being the most useful resource they find builds trust before they even walk in the door.

7. "What to expect after microneedling: the real recovery timeline" This is a top AI-generated answer topic. Patients want to know about redness, peeling, when they can wear makeup, and when they'll see results. Be specific about day-by-day recovery. Avoid vague language like "mild downtime." The clinics that give real information attract patients who show up prepared and satisfied.

8. "Botox aftercare: what NOT to do in the first 24 hours" The negative framing ("what not to do") outperforms "how to care for" in search because it's how people actually phrase the query. Write a post that covers exercise, lying down, alcohol, and pressure on injection sites with real reasoning — not just a list of rules.

9. "How long before my event should I get filler?" Timeline questions spike around wedding season, prom season, and the holidays. A post that gives concrete guidance — two weeks for first-time filler, one week for touch-ups — answers a question patients are already asking and encourages them to book far enough in advance to see you.

Before-and-After and Results Posts — Social Proof Meets SEO

Before-and-after content ranks well, gets shared, and converts. Pair real patient results with the search terms patients use when they're looking for proof a treatment works.

10. "Before and after: what 40 units of Botox actually looks like" Specificity is what makes this post rankable and trustworthy. "40 units" is a concrete term. Pair a real result with a breakdown of where units were placed and what the patient's goals were. Patients who find this are looking for social proof, and you're delivering it with context.

11. "What laser skin resurfacing results actually look like after 90 days" Tying results to local context — heat, humidity, sun exposure in your area — differentiates this from generic national content a patient might find. It signals that you understand the local environment and its effect on their skin, which matters to patients who live near your practice.

AI-Question Posts — The Category Most Med Spas Are Missing

AI engines like ChatGPT and Perplexity now answer questions that used to drive traffic to blogs. The way to compete: write posts specifically designed to be the source AI pulls from.

12. "Is Botox safe? What the research actually shows" Safety questions are among the most-asked injectable topics in AI chatbots. A clear, factual post citing peer-reviewed outcomes, FDA approvals, and realistic risk profiles written in plain language is exactly the kind of source AI engines cite. This post builds credibility, earns citations, and attracts patients who need reassurance before they'll book.

Write for the Question, Not the Topic

The pattern across all 12 of these: they match a specific question a specific patient asks at a specific point in the buying process. That's what separates content that converts from content that just exists.

Start with two or three posts from the cost and comparison sections — those capture the highest-intent traffic. Build toward the AI-question category over time.

Frequently asked questions

What kinds of blog posts work best for med spa bookings?+–

Cost posts, comparison posts, and treatment-prep guides outperform general educational content because they reach people who have already decided they want a treatment and are just picking a provider. Those readers are one click away from booking — your job is to be the most useful answer they find.

How often should a med spa publish blog content?+–

Two to four posts per month is a sustainable pace for most med spas. Consistency matters more than volume. A well-researched post published twice a month will outperform a rushed post every week. Focus each post on one specific question with clear buyer intent rather than broad educational topics.

Should med spa blog posts target local keywords?+–

Yes, always. Generic posts about Botox compete with WebMD and national chains you cannot outrank. Local posts — 'lip filler cost near me' or 'best microneedling in your city' — compete in a much smaller pool and attract patients who are actually near your practice and ready to book.

Do blog posts help med spas show up in AI answers like ChatGPT?+–

Yes, especially posts structured in a question-and-answer format with direct, specific answers near the top. AI engines reward content that clearly answers the query without burying the answer in marketing copy. FAQ schema markup on those posts increases the odds even further.

How long should a med spa blog post be?+–

Long enough to fully answer the question, short enough to stay readable. For cost and comparison posts, 600 to 900 words is usually enough. For 'what to expect' or multi-step prep guides, 900 to 1,400 words gives you room to be genuinely useful. Word count is not the goal — completeness is.

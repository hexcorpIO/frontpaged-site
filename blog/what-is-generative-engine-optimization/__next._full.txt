1c:T1dd2,<h2>The Short Answer First</h2>
<p>Generative engine optimization (GEO) is how you get your med spa cited inside AI-generated answers instead of just ranked in a list of links.</p>
<p>When someone opens ChatGPT and types &quot;best place for lip fillers near me,&quot; they&#39;re not getting ten blue links. They&#39;re getting a paragraph that names specific providers. GEO is how you become one of those names.</p>
<p>That&#39;s the whole concept. Everything else is execution.</p>
<hr>
<h2>Why This Is Different From What You&#39;ve Done Before</h2>
<p>Traditional SEO is a ranking game. You optimize pages, build links, and climb Google&#39;s results until you&#39;re on page one. Users see your link, click it, and (hopefully) book.</p>
<p>GEO is a citation game. AI engines — Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT, Perplexity — read thousands of sources, synthesize them into a direct answer, and pick a handful of businesses to mention. If your content is structured the way these models prefer, you get mentioned. If it&#39;s written like a brochure, you don&#39;t.</p>
<p>The stakes are real. A 2025 study found that AI Overviews appear for roughly 47% of health and beauty searches. That&#39;s nearly half your potential patients seeing an AI-generated answer before a single link loads.</p>
<h3>What AI Engines Actually Want</h3>
<p>AI models aren&#39;t impressed by keyword density or meta tags the way old-school SEO tools were. They&#39;re looking for:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Clear, direct answers</strong> to specific questions (&quot;How long does Botox last?&quot; gets a better response from a page that answers it in two sentences than one that buries the answer in a 1,200-word essay)</li>
<li><strong>Structured data</strong> — FAQ schema, local business schema, review schema — that tells the model what your content is about</li>
<li><strong>Consistent business information</strong> — name, address, phone, and hours that match across Google, Yelp, RealSelf, Healthgrades, and your own site</li>
<li><strong>Credible citations</strong> — local press mentions, partnerships with dermatologists, links from medical directories</li>
<li><strong>Genuine reviews</strong> — not just star ratings, but text reviews that mention specific treatments and outcomes</li>
</ul>
<hr>
<h2>A Concrete Med Spa Example</h2>
<p>Say your Botox page currently reads: <em>&quot;At [Spa Name], we offer world-class Botox treatments delivered by our experienced team in a luxurious environment.&quot;</em></p>
<p>An AI engine reads that and learns almost nothing useful. It can&#39;t cite you for &quot;how much does Botox cost near me&quot; or &quot;how many units of Botox for forehead lines&quot; because you haven&#39;t answered those questions.</p>
<p>Now imagine you restructure that page with a section that reads:</p>
<p><em>&quot;Botox at our clinic typically ranges from $12 to $15 per unit. A forehead treatment uses 10 to 30 units depending on your anatomy, so most patients spend $150 to $450. Results last 3 to 4 months. We recommend a follow-up at week two to assess results.&quot;</em></p>
<p>That&#39;s a citable answer. When someone asks ChatGPT &quot;how much does Botox cost near me,&quot; your page is a candidate for the response. Add <a href="/blog/faq-schema-for-med-spas">FAQ schema markup</a> around that content and you&#39;ve made it even easier for AI models to extract and attribute it.</p>
<p>This is GEO in practice — not a technical trick, but a content strategy shift.</p>
<hr>
<h2>How GEO Fits Into Your Existing Marketing</h2>
<p>You don&#39;t have to throw out your SEO work. A lot of it still matters because AI engines use traditional ranking signals to gauge credibility. A page that ranks well on Google is more likely to be pulled into an AI Overview than one buried on page four.</p>
<p>What GEO adds on top:</p>
<p><strong>Content restructuring.</strong> Convert service pages from marketing copy to resource pages. Answer the real questions patients ask — &quot;does laser hair removal hurt?&quot;, &quot;what&#39;s the difference between Sculptra and Juvederm?&quot;, &quot;how many sessions does CoolSculpting take?&quot;</p>
<p><strong>Schema markup.</strong> Technical tags that tell AI engines your content format, your business details, and your FAQs. Read our <a href="/blog/med-spa-seo-2026-guide">full med spa SEO guide</a> for how schema fits into your broader strategy.</p>
<p><strong>Authority signals.</strong> AI engines weight citations from recognized sources. A mention in a local publication, a profile on RealSelf, or a quote from your lead injector in a health article all help.</p>
<p><strong>Review content.</strong> Encourage patients to write reviews that mention specific treatments. &quot;Amazing Botox results&quot; is less useful than &quot;I came in for 20 units of Botox on my forehead and the results were natural and lasted four months.&quot; The specifics get picked up.</p>
<hr>
<h2>The Local Angle: Every Market Is Competitive</h2>
<p>Every city has dozens of med spas competing for the same patients. When someone asks Perplexity for laser treatment recommendations in your area, a handful of names will come up. Those names won&#39;t be randomly selected — they&#39;ll be the practices with the most structured, credible, question-answering content online.</p>
<p>That&#39;s actually good news if you move early. GEO is still new enough that most med spas haven&#39;t done it intentionally. The practices that structure their content now, build their schema, and earn consistent local citations will own those AI responses for years.</p>
<p>Understanding <a href="/blog/how-ai-search-recommends-med-spas">how AI search recommends med spas</a> gets into the ranking mechanics in more depth — worth reading once you&#39;ve got the GEO basics down.</p>
<hr>
<h2>Where to Start</h2>
<p>If you&#39;re running a med spa and want to show up in AI answers, here&#39;s a practical first pass:</p>
<ol>
<li><strong>Audit your top 5 service pages.</strong> Read them like a patient would. Do they answer the questions patients actually ask? If not, add a plain-language Q&amp;A section to each.</li>
<li><strong>Add FAQ schema to those pages.</strong> This is a half-day technical task, but the payoff is outsized.</li>
<li><strong>Check your NAP consistency.</strong> Search your business name and make sure the address, phone, and hours are identical everywhere they appear online.</li>
<li><strong>Ask for specific reviews.</strong> Coach happy patients to mention the treatment, the result, and the timeframe.</li>
<li><strong>Get one or two local citations.</strong> Reach out to a local health blogger, submit a profile to a medical directory, or see if your chamber of commerce has a member spotlight.</li>
</ol>
<p>None of this requires a complete website overhaul. Small, targeted changes to content and structure compound quickly in AI systems.</p>
<hr>

<p>GEO isn&#39;t a future concern — it&#39;s deciding who gets recommended right now. Every week your service pages go unanswered, a competitor&#39;s structured content is getting cited instead.</p>
